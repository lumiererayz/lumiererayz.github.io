We jointly create new knowledge

We believe that persons with relevant ideas, skills and commitment need to engage with the underlying causes of issues that children and young people face. CDIN promote knowledge transfer and exchange among children (boy/girl) and youth care professionals and other persons who plays a role in the daily lives of children and young persons. Our work focuses on early and late adolescent age range, in the areas of: Rehabilitation for endangered and vulnerable children who has been exposed to various social vices.

Training

CDIN has built up extensive experience in delivering training on various subjects relevant to psychosocial development of children and youth. Our training sessions are always interactive in set-up, leaving ample room for inputs and feedback from participants. Every training is tailored to the needs and wishes of the contracting organization. Please be aware that CDIN cannot offer any training for free. We expect the contracting organization to cover all costs, including fees and other related costs for CDIN trainers. For an overview of our current training-portfolio please click on the button below. 

Research

CDIN believes that research is an important means to understand children’s problems and to improve projects and programmes to address such problems. We pride ourselves in being able to implement high quality research, using both quantitative and qualitative tools, which are based on up-to-date theory, but always practice oriented. Our research is guided by the UN  Convention on the Rights of the Child and follows strict ethical guidelines. We also have developed several research tools and have delivered training on how to involve children and youth as researchers themselves. 

Advice

CDIN has over twenty years of international experience in the areas of programme development, policy advice, training, and research related  to psychosocial child and youth development. CDIN staff can be made available for short-term consultancy assignments, such as evaluations of projects, research, training, technical support or organizational and policy advice

Partnership

CDIN is always looking to build new partnerships. We bring together people from different educational backgrounds, fields of work and walks of life; people who share a passion for, and interest in things that are important for and to children and young people. We do this because a holistic approach to child and youth development is the best way to address the underlying causes of the issues they face.
